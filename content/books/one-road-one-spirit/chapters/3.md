March 8, 2026 – A Quiet Day by the Ocean

Today we drove toward Bradenton and stopped at a Vietnamese tropical outdoor restaurant. The place was beautifully landscaped, filled with palms, fruit trees, and colorful tropical plants. It must have been a small paradise when everything was thriving. But walking through the garden, I noticed something that made me a little sad. Many of the fruit trees and tropical plants had been burned by the freeze that came through a few weeks ago. Their leaves were brown and curled, the branches looking tired from the cold that Florida rarely sees.

Even so, the place still carried a peaceful charm.

We ordered a simple meal — vermicelli noodles and papaya salad, the kind of food that feels light and fresh in warm weather. After eating, we decided to continue toward the beach.

Doua wanted to check out a fishing spot he had been hearing about. His online fishing friends had been talking about how good the catch had been lately, and curiosity pulled us toward the water.

Now I am sitting quietly in the car next to the ocean.

A cool breeze drifts in from the Gulf. The air smells faintly of salt and sea. We parked behind a cluster of palm trees, their long leaves swaying gently in the wind. Through the spaces between the trees, I can see the sunlight dancing on the surface of the water. The reflections shimmer like thousands of tiny mirrors scattered across the ocean.

On the bridge nearby, fishermen stand patiently with their lines cast into the water. Some are walking back toward their cars, carrying buckets and coolers, their day slowly winding down.

For me, this moment feels simple and peaceful.

Today is another day of leisure. There is no rush, no pressure, no work waiting to be done. Just the sound of the wind, the sight of the ocean, and the quiet comfort of being here.

Sometimes the greatest luxury in life is simply this — the freedom to pause, breathe, and enjoy the moment.
